Hello! Welcome to my site. <span style="color: #1a6b3c;"><strong>My name is Katie, and I am a Postdoctoral Research Scholar</strong></span> in the Department of Parks, Recreation and Tourism Management at North Carolina State University and a Postdoctoral Research Scientist in the Department of Epidemiology Mailman School of Public Health at Columbia University. I am based in Brooklyn, New York. 
I am also a postdoctoral fellow in the [Center for Geospatial Analytics](https://cnr.ncsu.edu/geospatial/about/) at NC State, member of the [Built Environment and Health Research Group](https://beh.columbia.edu/), and a fellow in the [Columbia Population Research Center](https://cprc.columbia.edu/).

I use <span style="color: #1a6b3c;"><strong>my training in epidemiology, GIS, environmental health, and community health</strong></span> to conduct research focused on how <span style="color: #1a6b3c;"><strong>urban policy and design impact physical activity and injury outcomes related to active transportation</strong></span>.

Currently, I am most excited about my ongoing work to generate evidence and develop GIS translation tools that support the equitable expansion and sustainability of the Open Streets for Schools Program in NYC.  

My research is guided by my belief that <span style="color: #1a6b3c;"><strong>all communities should have access to safe opportunities for walking, bicycling, playing</strong></span>, and all other forms of physical activity.

I would love to chat with anyone about my work or or opportunities to collaborate. Feel free to email me directly.
